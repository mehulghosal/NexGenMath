In mathematics, a matrix (plural: matrices) is a rectangular array of numbers, symbols, or expressions, arranged in rows and columns. For example, the following are matrices: $$ \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 & 8 \\ 5 & 24 & 18 \end{bmatrix} $$ $$\begin{bmatrix} x & x^2 & x^3 \\ x^2 & y^3 & z^4 \end{bmatrix}$$ $$\begin{bmatrix} ln(x) & e^x & y \\ 5x & 1/y & 22 \end{bmatrix}$$ The number of rows in a matrix is its length (denoted as L) and the number of columns in a matrix is its width (denoted as W). The size of the matrix is $$length \ \ \textbf{X} \ \ width$$ Thus, the size of all three matrices above is $$2 \ \textbf{X} \ 3$$.
